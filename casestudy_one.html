A UX/UI Design Bootcamp Case Study

Extensive UI Redesign for Medical Non-Profit

Project Overview

Description

Angel Flight Soars is a non-profit organization that arranges free air transportation for medical patients who would otherwise have difficulties traveling for their medical appointments. However, the organization’s current website design is clunky and outdated, with confusing information architecture and lacking a responsive mobile version.

Our design team decided to implement a complete overhaul of the UI, updating all webpages for both desktop and mobile and applied an updated information architecture system.

The Problem

After speaking with the stakeholders of Angel Flight Soars, we identified three main user groups: medical patients, volunteer pilots, and healthcare workers.
However, after conducting a usability analysis following all three user flows we encountered many user problems. All users were not able to quickly locate the mission and impact, leading to confusion and initial distrust of the organization’s validity.

Medical patients and pilots had difficulty gathering information surrounding the process of applying for the organization’s services, and had many questions that the site did not provide answers for.

The Solution

  • Streamline the process of applying as a medical patient and volunteer

  • Create a delightful user experience while navigating the Angel Flight Soars’ user interface

  • Organize all web pages so users can easily find information, and gather answers to usual questions

  • Have user proceed feeling empowered with knowledge of the organization.

Design Process

Empathize

We empathized with a medical patient attempting to apply for Angel Flight Soars’ services on the current site.

Research

A group of designers can have many ideas when approaching a complete user interface redesign, and so to get started on the correct path we reached out to Angel Flight Soars.

The redesign should be professional, clean, and modern. With this, we began propelling Angel Flight Soars’ online presence into the future.

Ideation

The usability analysis indicated the second navigation function on webpages caused much of the user confusion, and so our current site map did away with the function, relying on the main menu bar for site navigation.

Visualization

Hi-Fidelity testing revealed that our users were able to complete the intended user flows with no issues.

Development

After user feedback, some minor design changes we implemented include:

  • Refining the primary navigation and footer elements to increase visibility

  • Aligned elements to a grid to ensure both desktop and mobile incorporate responsive web design

Results

Our hope with this redesign is to give medical patients and pilots a sense of empowerment and security when working with such a great organization. We expect more website traffic, with greater user retention rates and a delightful experience when researching Angel Flight Soars online, all in the aim of increasing awareness and reaching those in need of their services.

Our redesign came at the perfect time, as Angel Flight Soars was preparing to update their online presence. We have sent our design to the organization and patiently wait to see what elements will be included in the final product!
